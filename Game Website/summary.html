Introduction

Krishnakumar Kunnath, popularly known as KK or Kay Kay, shows a lot of leaning towards Kishore Kumar and RD Burman in his musical disposition. His melodious voice and penchant for love songs touches a chord with most of his listeners, making him stand tall among all the talented singers of Bollywood.

Early Life

Popularly known as KK or Kay Kay, Krishnakumar Kunnath was brought up in New Delhi. His early years began in Mount St. Mary's School, Delhi Cantt., and he then moved on to graduate from Kirori Mal College, Delhi University. After this, he moved on to Mumbai to start his career as a singer. In his formative years, he started as ad jingle singer when he presented his demo tapes to Louis Banks, Ranjit Barot and Leslie Lewis in 1994, just to get a break. Soon, he got a break and sang a jingle for Santogen Suitings from UTV. Within a short duration of four years, he sang over 3500 jingles in eleven Indian languages. He credits Leslie Lewis for being his mentor in the world of advertising jingles. KK soon became a playback singer for A. R. Rahman's hit songs like College Style (Tamil version with Hariharan), and Hello Dr (Hindi version) in the 1996 Tamil movie Kadhal Desam. By 1997, he sang Strawberry Kannae for another Tamil musical film Minsara Kanavu. He also sang the Hindi version called Strawberry Aankhen with Kavita Paudwal.

Awards won by KK

KK is one of the most celebrated playback singers in the Indian music industry. He has won several awards and accolades for his soulful and versatile singing. Here are some of the awards won by KK:

  1. Filmfare Awards: KK has won four Filmfare Awards for Best Male Playback Singer. He won his first Filmfare Award in 2006 for the song "Tadap Tadap" from the movie "Hum Dil De Chuke Sanam." He won his second Filmfare Award in 2008 for the song "Khuda Jaane" from the movie "Bachna Ae Haseeno." He won his third and fourth Filmfare Awards in 2009 and 2010 for the songs "Paa" from the movie "Paa" and "Zindagi Do Pal Ki" from the movie "Kites," respectively.
  2. IIFA Awards: KK has won three International Indian Film Academy Awards for Best Male Playback Singer. He won his first IIFA Award in 2006 for the song "Tadap Tadap" from the movie "Hum Dil De Chuke Sanam." He won his second and third IIFA Awards in 2009 and 2011 for the songs "Paa" from the movie "Paa" and "Zindagi Do Pal Ki" from the movie "Kites," respectively.
  3. Zee Cine Awards: KK has won two Zee Cine Awards for Best Male Playback Singer. He won his first Zee Cine Award in 2006 for the song "Aashayein" from the movie "Iqbal." He won his second Zee Cine Award in 2008 for the song "Khuda Jaane" from the movie "Bachna Ae Haseeno.”
  4. Star Screen Awards: KK has won two Star Screen Awards for Best Male Playback Singer. He won his first Star Screen Award in 2006 for the song "Tadap Tadap" from the movie "Hum Dil De Chuke Sanam." He won his second Star Screen Award in 2009 for the song "Paa" from the movie "Paa.”
